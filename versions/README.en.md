## <a name="food"></a> 🍽️ Food

### <a name="food-com"></a> Rice (Cơm)

#### [Cơm gà bà Thuỷ (chicken rice)](https://share.google/lpQ7m7AdChxZhCmBo)

- **Address:** 279 Chính Hữu
- **Price:** 45k – 70k
- Tasty, high-quality chicken rice. A family-run spot; the chicken quality and price suit tourists and anyone who values quality.
- My favorite dish is the boiled, hand-shredded chicken, served with greens, cucumber, tomato and kimchi.
- When dining with family, order the soup separately — it's better that way. The seaweed soup comes with big, delicious shrimp.

#### [Cơm gà Hồng Ngọc (chicken rice)](https://share.google/dB9BS4xg5DP9phOMa)

- **Address:** 193 Nguyễn Chí Thanh
- **Price:** 45k – 70k
- Famous chicken rice in Đà Nẵng. Spacious, can handle large groups.

### <a name="food-bun"></a> Noodles (Bún)

#### [Bún Quậy Phú Quốc](https://g.page/bunquaydacsanphuquoc?share)

- **Address:** 61 Huỳnh Thúc Kháng
- **Price:** 40k – 60k / bowl
- Fresh noodles made on the spot, fresh seafood, delicious. Great for going solo or in a small group.

#### Bún Bò Huế (Huế-style beef noodle soup)

- **Address:** Alley 123 Nguyễn Thị Minh Khai, Đà Nẵng
- **Price:** 40k – 60k
- Morning only.
- Coming back from the beach and downing a bowl of this is the best! :))

#### Bún chả cá 109 (fish-cake noodle soup)

- **Address:** [109 Nguyễn Chí Thanh, Hải Châu 1, Hải Châu](https://goo.gl/maps/QCTbgwgqLbUwUUFK6)
- **Price:** around 30k

#### [Bún Chả Hà Nội](https://goo.gl/maps/GSWwQxsTqqd69fnq8)

- **Address:** the intersection across from 86 Huy Cận, Hòa Cường Nam, Hải Châu, Đà Nẵng
- **Price:** 20k / bowl
- The owner is from Đà Nẵng but keeps the Hà Nội bún chả flavor. Reasonable price, just enough to fill you up. My older brother eats it almost every morning.
- **Note:**
  - This is a breakfast spot.
  - After your bowl, cross to the café opposite — [White Cafe](https://www.foody.vn/da-nang/white-cafe-86-huy-can) — for a salt coffee to complete the morning combo.

#### Bún Cô Phú

- **Address:** 78 Khúc Hạo
- **Price:** 20k – 40k / bowl
- **Note:** open in the afternoon/evening.

### <a name="food-my-quang"></a> Mì Quảng

#### [Mỳ Quảng Bà Mua](https://www.facebook.com/MonNgonMyQuang)

- **Address:** [19-21 Trần Bình Trọng, Phước Ninh, Hải Châu](https://goo.gl/maps/Wy4EfWPDSc9nqg3g8)
- **Price:** from 45k
- Best eaten at the Trần Bình Trọng branch. Despite several branches, the seasoning isn't consistent — even the chili jar at the Hồ Nghinh branch is totally different from the original.

#### [Mỳ Quảng Sứa Hồng Vân (with jellyfish)](https://www.foody.vn/da-nang/mi-quang-sua-hong-van)

- **Address:** 59 Lê Hồng Phong

#### [Mỳ Quảng Vỉa Hè (sidewalk stall)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/3UofRQcmJy12LZc37)

- **Address:** the intersection by the Sơn Trà fiscal-control office and Hà Thân market =)), diagonally across from the park
- **Price:** 15k – 20k
- Early morning only (6 – 8 AM); usually sold out by 8.
- Incredibly good Mì Quảng (haven't found anywhere better or cheaper).
- The lady rides in from Quảng Nam every morning to sell; the herbs are countryside-fresh, so pile them on.
- The bowl is huge — if you're a woman, ask for less noodles, because she has a generous hand @@

#### [Mỳ Tôm Mực Thông Thành (shrimp & squid noodles)](https://g.page/bunquaydacsanphuquoc?share)

- **Address:** Lot 17 Hoàng Sa, Sơn Trà, Đà Nẵng (across from Mân Thái beach)
- **Price:** 40k – 60k / bowl
- Amazing after SUP or a swim. The second-best time: after a loop around Sơn Trà, then stopping in to eat.
- The squid is super fresh and delicious. If you're worried about staining your mouth, ask them to remove the ink sac (they usually leave it in to sweeten the broth).

### <a name="food-banh-canh"></a> Bánh Canh

#### [Bánh Canh Cá Cô Cúc (with fish)](https://www.foody.vn/da-nang/banh-canh-ca-nuong)

- **Address:** 82-84 Bùi Dương Lịch
- **Price:** 12k / bowl
- Afternoon/evening only.
- Locals call it "pig bánh canh" because they cook it in a giant pot and ladle it out roughly, like feeding pigs =)))
- Self-service: queue to grab your bowl, then call to pay when done — it's so busy they don't do table service.
- You can add nem chua, chả and grilled mackerel on the side. A glass of sugarcane juice makes it perfect. It's hard to spend more than 50k no matter how much you eat.

#### [Bánh Canh Cá Lóc Hiền](https://www.foody.vn/da-nang/quan-hien-dac-san-quang-tri-huynh-thuc-khang)

- **Address:** 134 Huỳnh Thúc Kháng
- **Price:** 25k / bowl; nem, chả 5k
- Serves Quảng Trị-style bánh canh, bánh ướt with pork offal, and bún mắm.
- Delicious and spicy. If you like scallions, order extra to "cure a cold" and it's perfect.

#### [Bánh Canh Rong Biển (with seaweed)](https://share.google/89aLSOasmMfqpH5S0)

- **Address:** 92 Huỳnh Thúc Kháng
- **Price:** 30k / bowl
- Serves seaweed bánh canh (with tapioca, brown-rice or wheat dough). Delicious, open all day.

## <a name="sight-seeing"></a> 🚶 Walks & Sightseeing

### Walking on Sơn Trà mountain

- **Route:** from the ["Sơn Trà Peninsula Guard Station"](https://goo.gl/maps/TxacQaqZGgYsmAzv8) to [Vọng Cảnh Hill](https://goo.gl/maps/tAZFKyfau9j63B1LA)
- On an automatic scooter, you'll be stopped at this station and not allowed higher. You need a manual bike to continue up to the radar station or Bàn Cờ peak.
- This stretch is great to walk because:
  - Little traffic (a different direction from the touristy Linh Ứng temple).
  - You may spot langurs foraging, or monkeys and various birds.
  - There's a newly planted myrtle-flower path.
  - Views over the city. Walking up to Vọng Cảnh Hill is about 2 – 3 km; grabbing a coconut, enjoying the view, then heading down is a perfect plan =))
  - Go in the morning when it's cool; afternoons facing the sunset get very hot =))
  - If you're lazy, grab a coffee, walk 400 m, and head back down — that works too :3

### Walking in the city

- **Route:** Nguyễn Chí Thanh – Lê Lợi
- Best in the morning, around 7 – 9 AM.
- Start at 129 Nguyễn Chí Thanh (i3 café), walk to Nguyễn Du, turn left and loop back along Lê Lợi (about 1.5 – 2 km).
- The i3 café has a sidewalk (with a huge, pretty tree) where you can park your bike (their milk coffee is good, try it if you like).
- This route has lots of trees, plenty of shade, sidewalks, and everyday street life to watch. You'll pass the bún chả cá at 199 Nguyễn Chí Thanh and Cà phê Long (above).

### Walking along the Hàn River

- **Address:** Bạch Đằng
- Cool and lively at night; a bit empty and sunny in the morning.
- You can park at APEC Park and wander freely. If you're thirsty, there are vending machines.

### Walking on the Nguyễn Văn Trỗi pedestrian bridge

- **Getting there:** there are 2 ways up to the bridge on foot:
  1. At the foot of Trần Thị Lý bridge (busy in the evening, with drink and snack stalls). Just park your bike and go — nobody seems to watch it.
  2. On foot from the green-island area (not recommended: it's deserted and nobody watches the bikes).
- At sunset it's a great walk with lovely scenery; the space is wide, cool and beautiful.
- At night Gen Z comes out to eat, drink and sing... Overall fun but still polite and quiet, in small groups on the bridge.

### Riding around by motorbike

- If you want to cruise around, some tree-lined, shady streets are:
  - Nguyễn Chí Thanh (one-way), from the Lê Duẩn junction to the end.
  - Phan Châu Trinh (one-way), from the Nguyễn Văn Linh junction to the end.
  - Thanh Thủy, from the Ông Ích Khiêm junction out to 3/2.

### Swimming at the beach

- **Address:** Phạm Văn Đồng beach
- Swimming in summer is ideal: fresh water and air, fewer people, and cleaner than in the afternoon.
- Park at [lot no. 3](https://goo.gl/maps/vHKAMjMgkCVrSaxt5) (the farthest) since it's less crowded — bike 4k + swim 2k/person.
- Go before sunrise (around 4:30 – 5:30 AM) to watch the sun come up over the sea beside Sơn Trà mountain and understand why it's so amazing.

### Sunset & the (tiny) wonder park

- **Address:** across from [Đà Nẵng Golden Bay Hotel](https://g.page/wonder-park-danang?share)
- To catch the sunset, come when the sun is just dipping behind the mountain (around 5:30 – 6 PM) in summer.
- There's an embankment here where people sit to watch the sunset by the foot of Thuận Phước bridge.
- The wonder park is nearby, so you can drop by too.
- In the morning it's also cool and airy, perfect for buying a coffee and chilling.
- After the sunset, a bowl of Cô Cúc's bánh canh (above) warms the soul; then an evening stroll along the Hàn River and off to a good night's sleep.

## <a name="treaking"></a> ⛰️ Hiking

### [Ghềnh Bàng drinks stall](https://maps.app.goo.gl/HviFT5R8kU6bS7p39)

- **Address:** Ghềnh Bàng, Sơn Trà
- **Price:** 10k – 30k (parking, drinks)
- Gather at the Ghềnh Bàng drinks stall. At the end of the road up to the Intercon you'll see a guard post; to the right there's a path to a small café (with lots of almond trees).
- Park and ask around; there'll be a trail into the forest. After about 15 – 20 minutes of hiking you reach Ghềnh Bàng. The trail is rough, so bring water, a hat and sneakers.
- The forest is quite cool; down at Ghềnh Bàng it's wild and beautiful. You can bring some food to snack and rest.
- Avoid teasing the monkeys, they're fairly aggressive. Avoid swimming, as there are no lifeguards.

### [Hải Vân Quan forest trail](https://maps.app.goo.gl/ACPWVrfCPL6bnyoCA)

- **Address:** Hải Vân terrace — Hải Vân Quan
- On reaching Hải Vân Quan, park / leave your bike.
- Beside it there's a road with a barrier you can easily step over :3
- A ~30-minute hike through the mountain, with lovely views.
- Be careful, the forest trail can be dangerous (animals). Go in a group and bring water, a hat and sneakers.

## <a name="hoi-an"></a> 🏮 Hội An

### [Sound Of Silence Coffee Shop](https://maps.app.goo.gl/cBDFqon9jb3LWUKZ9)

- **Address:** 40 Nguyễn Phan Vinh, Hội An
- **Price:** 50k – 100k
- If I had to pick one café in Hội An, I'd pick this. Beautiful garden and a direct sea view from up high.
- If you want a peaceful spot to gaze at the sea and hear the waves, this is the ideal place. It can get windy in the afternoon, so bring a jacket.

### [Cà phê Hoa Lúa](https://maps.app.goo.gl/GpR6HVRydNHiHfTg9)

- **Address:** An Mỹ ward, Hội An Đông, Đà Nẵng
- **Price:** 20k – 50k
- Nice views, cool and airy space, great for watching the sunset and strolling through the rice fields. Breathe in the scent of ripe rice and an atmosphere the city won't have for a long time.
- Prettiest during the young-rice and ripe-rice seasons.

### [Cháo Nghêu Cô Gió (clam porridge)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/jtLLRHHd9jrBoBLJ8)

- **Address:** An Bàng beach, Hội An
- **Price:** 30k – 50k
- After coffee, a bowl of porridge warms the soul, then off into Hội An for a stroll — the perfect plan.
- Open all day (closes at 6 PM).
- As a Đà Nẵng local, the clams here don't seem as good as Đà Nẵng's sea clams =)) But in Hội An this dish is quite tasty, reasonably priced and popular.
- If you don't like farmed clams, order the bone porridge instead.

### [Ốc Hương Sả Tắc (snails with lemongrass)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/vFqEZ5S7RCACJAXZ8)

- **Address:** 155 Tôn Đức Thắng, Hội An Tây, Đà Nẵng
- **Price:** 80k – 150k / dish
- Evening only, from 4 PM.
- The best snail spot in Hội An. Their babylonia snails stir-fried with lemongrass and kumquat are super tasty, fresh and big, at a reasonable price. There's also snails with coconut, razor clams with tamarind, grilled scallops with scallion oil... It's busy, so go early to avoid the wait.
